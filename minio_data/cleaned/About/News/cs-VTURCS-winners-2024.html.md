# Undergraduate research brings accolades
Students, faculty, and alumni gathered to present and celebrate a semester full of research and innovation.
Students presenting their work to faculty and alumni judges at the 2024 Virginia Tech Undergraduate Research in Computer Science exhibition and competition. Photo by Margaret Ellis for Virginia Tech.
A range of projects took top slots in the 2024 annual Virginia Tech Undergraduate Research in Computer Science competition. The projects were judged by faculty and alumni.
## Capstone project category
**1st place (tie)**
  * "HokiePark": Eugene Min, Adalia Truong, Ken Lin, Allison Deaton, An Truong
  * "NanoBrick: Lightweight Segmentation and Classification of LEGO Bricks for a Mobile Kit Management Platform": Jay Katyan, Samuel Garver, Ali Hassan, Ramez Ahment, Adel Eltaher


**2nd place**
  * "Whack-A-Math": Daniel Tolessa, Devika Mittal, Hana Shalaby, Magdeldin Ahmed, Gabriele Boni


**3rd place**
  * "AutoNotes": Harry Leverone, Mark Carroll, Ryan Teer, Stephen Ippolito, Nayeli Zurita Herbas


## Research category
**1st place**
  * "Predicting VR Gaze Data from System Performance": Ken Lin, Paul Maynard, Bo Ji, Brendan David-John


**2nd place**
  * "Modernizing the Academic Journey: A User-Centric Plan of Study Tool for MS and PhD Students in CS": Yasemin C. Tanyu, Peiqing Guo


**3rd place**
  * "Red is Sus: Predicting the Accuracy of Broadband Submissions": Zhouwei Wen, Brooke Ritter


Teams presented their work via poster boards and answered judges questions. Photo by Sally Hamouda for Virginia Tech.
